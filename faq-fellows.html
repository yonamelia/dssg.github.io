FAQs for Summer Fellowship Applicants

Apply to be a Summer Fellow

How do I apply?

Fill out the application form by February 1, 2014 and make sure your references also fill out the recommendation form

What kind of fellows are you looking for?

We're looking for strong graduate (and advanced undergraduate) students with computational, quantitative, and analytics skills who care about making a social impact. Last year's fellows included people with Computer Science, Machine Learning, Statistics, Economics, Applied Math, Physics, Linguistics, Sociology, and Public Policy degrees

When does the fellowship start and finish?

We'll start end of May and finish end of August. We're working on figuring out multiple start dates that can accomodate different end of quarter/semester dates

Is it a full-time program and do I have to be in Chicago?

Yes. And Yes.

Do I have to be a student to apply?

Not neccesarily but this is designed for current student or recent graduates. If you have finished a degree (undergrad, Masters, Phd) in the past couple of years, you should be ok to apply.

When do I hear back from the fellowship?

We'll review applications starting February 1 and will schedule interviews after we go through a round of reviewing. Final decisions will be sent out by March 15.

What projects am I going to be working on?

Call for Projects is still on-going. You can see projects from last year here. Projects will be in partnership with government and non profit organizations and will be in areas of education, healthcare, energy, community development, disaster relief, and public safety. We'll send out a specific list of projects with our decision by March 15.
